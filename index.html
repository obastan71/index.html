The Exposed Shepherd

A scene from a dramatic screenplay.

Logline: A respected community leader who preaches virtue is publicly exposed by his wife when his secret affair threatens to destroy their two families.


Characters:

  • PASTOR EPHRAIM (40s): Charismatic, well-dressed, a pillar of the community.
  • SARAH (40s): Ephraim's wife. Gentle and reserved, but with a fire burning beneath the surface.
  • MARK (40s): Ephraim's best friend.
  • DEBORAH (40s): Mark's wife.
  • CHURCH ELDER 1 (60s): The head of the board, stern and disappointed.
  • CHURCH ELDER 2 (50s): Younger, more visibly pained by the situation.

SCENE START

INT. PASTOR'S OFFICE - DAY

A pristine, book-lined office. PASTOR EPHRAIM sits at his large desk, a sermon open on his laptop. He is rehearsing his lines into a small microphone, his voice smooth and confident.

EPHRAIM

True leadership is not about being the shepherd. It's about being the lamb. It's about living a life of unimpeachable integrity, because our Lord sees all.

He smiles to himself, pleased with the line. A phone on the desk vibrates with a text message. He glances at it, a private smirk playing on his lips. He quickly types a reply. Just then, SARAH enters, a laundry basket in her arms.

SARAH

Putting these away. Mark asked if you were coming over later.

Ephraim's phone buzzes again. He quickly puts it face down on the desk.

EPHRAIM

Oh, I can't. The gala tonight. My message has to be... flawless.

SARAH

Your message on integrity?

He gives her a tight smile, trying to gauge her meaning.

EPHRAIM

Is there a problem, Sarah?

SARAH

Just an observation. The truth is timeless, isn't it?

She turns and exits, her words hanging in the air. Ephraim's relaxed posture stiffens.

INT. MARK AND DEBORAH'S HOUSE - DAY

MARK is in the kitchen, beaming as he talks on his phone, oblivious to DEBORAH, who is picking at her lunch with a worried expression.

MARK

I'm telling you, he's a true man of God. Ephraim gave me some of the best advice I've ever received on being a good husband. Said it was the most important calling a man could have.

He laughs into the phone.

MARK

Absolutely! See you tonight, brother!

He hangs up and puts an arm around Deborah.

MARK

The gala's going to be great. It's an honor to have a man like him guiding our community.

DEBORAH

He is... a guide.

Her voice is flat. Mark doesn't notice.

MARK

What's wrong, honey? You seem distant.

DEBORAH

Nothing. Just... the weight of it all. It's a lot to live up to.

Mark kisses her forehead.

MARK

We can only try our best. He's a true servant.

Deborah turns away, her face a mixture of shame and despair.

INT. CHURCH PARLOR - NIGHT

A fundraising gala is in full swing. Laughter and music fill the air. PASTOR EPHRAIM, confident and smiling, holds court with a small group of church elders. He sips from a glass of sparkling water.

EPHRAIM

And that, my friends, is the simple truth. Integrity isn’t what you preach; it’s what you live when no one is watching.

He gestures to the crowd, basking in their appreciative nods.

Across the room, SARAH watches him. Her face is a mask of calm, but her eyes are cold. MARK and DEBORAH, standing beside her, look uncomfortable.

MARK

Sarah, are you sure you're okay? You've barely touched your food.

SARAH

I’m fine, Mark. Just a little tired of the theatrics.

DEBORAH

She's been a rock through all of this. What an example of grace under pressure.

Sarah's gaze drifts from Deborah to Ephraim. A sharp pang of betrayal hits her.

SARAH

Grace? I've been a spectator to a sin. Not an example of grace.

Deborah and Mark exchange a confused look.

MARK

What are you talking about, Sarah?

Sarah ignores him. Her eyes lock with Ephraim's from across the room. He gives her a tight, polite smile.

Sarah begins to walk towards him, a single-minded determination in her stride. The chatter in the room dies down as people notice the tension. Ephraim's smile falters.

EPHRAIM

Sarah, darling. Everything alright?

He reaches for her arm, but she pulls away as if his touch is a flame.

SARAH

Don't call me that. We're doing this now. All of us.

She looks at Mark and Deborah, who have now caught up to her.

EPHRAIM

This isn't the place.

SARAH

Oh, I think it's the perfect place. It's in this very room, surrounded by people who hang on your every word, that you've built your kingdom of lies.

The crowd falls silent. All eyes are on them.

EPHRAIM

Sarah, please. Don't make a scene.

SARAH

You made the scene, Ephraim. For months. You made a mockery of your vows. Of my life. And of their marriage.

Her voice cracks, but her gaze remains firm on Deborah.

DEBORAH

What is she talking about?

Sarah's eyes fill with tears, but she doesn't break her stare.

SARAH

Ask your husband. Ask the man who preaches fidelity, yet spends his nights in hotel rooms with another man's wife. Your wife, Mark. Your wife.

The words hang in the air like a thunderclap. The room erupts in gasps and whispers. Mark's face goes pale. He turns slowly, fear and disbelief in his eyes, to look at Deborah.

She doesn't meet his gaze. She stares at the ground, her hands trembling, caught.

Ephraim stands frozen, his mask of confidence shattered. His mouth opens and closes, but no words come out. He's a man whose private sins have just been made brutally, irrevocably public.

INT. CHURCH OFFICE - NIGHT

Hours later. The gala is over. The silence in the office is deafening. EPHRAIM sits slumped in a chair, his tie undone, looking like a puppet with its strings cut. Two CHURCH ELDERS sit across from him, their faces a mixture of disappointment and sadness.

CHURCH ELDER 1

The board has met, and the decision is final. The evidence is incontrovertible. We have received statements from both Sarah and Deborah.

Ephraim says nothing. He simply stares at the floor.

CHURCH ELDER 2

We are all deeply saddened, Ephraim. You have done so much for this community. But the truth is, we cannot have a leader who preaches one thing and practices another. You've caused a great deal of pain and scandal.

EPHRAIM

I... I have.

His voice is a whisper.

CHURCH ELDER 1

For the integrity of the church, you are hereby suspended indefinitely. All pastoral duties are to be ceased, effective immediately.

Ephraim looks up, the reality finally hitting him.

EPHRAIM

So that's it? I'm out?

CHURCH ELDER 2

We will pray for you, brother. But this is a matter that must be handled with truth and accountability. As you said so eloquently in your own sermon... "our Lord sees all."

Ephraim looks at his hands, trembling slightly. The words he had just preached have come back to expose him.

SCENE END
